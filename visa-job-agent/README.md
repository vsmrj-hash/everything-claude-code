## SPONSORSHIP PROBABILITY FRAMEWORK

Use this to decide whether to apply when sponsorship isn't explicit:

### 🟢 HIGH PROBABILITY (Apply immediately)
- Job description says "visa sponsorship" or "work authorization"
- Company has employees from multiple countries (check LinkedIn)
- Company is HQ'd or large office in target country
- Company has sponsored before (check Glassdoor, LinkedIn profiles of employees)
- Company explicitly says "open to international candidates"

### 🟡 MEDIUM PROBABILITY (Apply + do research first)
- Large multinational company (>500 employees)
- Company recently expanded to target country
- Role is specialized / skill-shortage occupation
- Job is in tech sector in target country

### 🔴 LOW PROBABILITY (Skip unless dream job)
- Small local company (<50 employees)
- Non-tech industry in non-gateway city
- Job posting says "must have work authorization"
- Company has no international employees visible on LinkedIn

## VISA PATHWAYS REFERENCE

| Country | Visa Type | Key Requirement | Timeline |
|---------|-----------|----------------|----------|
| Canada | Global Talent Stream (Stream B) | Employer applies; 2-week processing | 2-4 weeks |
| Australia | TSS 482 Visa | Must be on MLTSSL or STSOL occupation list | 2-4 months |
| Germany | Skilled Worker Visa §18a | Job offer + qualification recognition | 2-4 months |
| Netherlands | Kennismigrant | Salary threshold (~€5,008/month 2024) | 2-4 weeks |
| Ireland | Critical Skills Employment Permit | Salary ≥€32k (≥€64k for most roles) | 4-8 weeks |
| Sweden | Work Permit | Employer initiates; union sign-off | 1-4 months |
| New Zealand | Accredited Employer Work Visa | Employer must be accredited | 4-8 weeks |
